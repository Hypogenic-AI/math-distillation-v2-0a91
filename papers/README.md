# Downloaded Papers

1. [The Latent Space of Equational Theories](berlioz_mellies_2026_latent_space.pdf)
   - Authors: Luis Berlioz, Paul-Andre Mellies
   - Year: 2026
   - arXiv: 2601.20759
   - Why relevant: Introduces Stone pairings as statistical fingerprints for equational theories. Shows that implications flow in structured ways through a latent space derived from satisfaction probabilities on random finite magmas. The Stone pairing approach could provide new features for predicting hard implications.

2. [The Equational Theories Project](tao_et_al_2025_etp.pdf)
   - Authors: Bolan, Breitner, Brox, Carneiro, ... Tao, et al. (33 authors)
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2512.07087
   - Why relevant: Foundational paper. Completely determines all 22M implications between 4694 equational laws on magmas using Lean. Documents all proof techniques: finite counterexamples (524 magmas refute 96.3% of false implications), syntactic rewriting, ATPs, canonizers, SAT solvers, linear/quadratic magma constructions.
